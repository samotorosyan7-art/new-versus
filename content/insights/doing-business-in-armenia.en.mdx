## Why Armenia, Now?

Armenia is experiencing a structural economic shift. The combination of a highly educated, multilingual workforce, aggressive IT tax incentives, and a geographic position bridging Europe, the CIS, and the Middle East makes it uniquely attractive for businesses seeking a stable, low-cost hub.

But "attractive" does not mean "simple." The regulatory nuances are real, and the cost of misunderstanding them can be significant.

## Regulatory Landscape

### Foreign Ownership
Unlike many jurisdictions in the region, Armenia permits **100% foreign ownership** of local companies. There are no restricted sectors for most business types, no profit repatriation restrictions, and no mandatory local partner requirements.

### Banking & Currency
The Armenian Dram (AMD) is freely convertible. Foreign currency accounts are permitted. The Central Bank of Armenia is generally considered stable and transparent, though they demand thorough documentation for new business registrations.

## Tax Incentives

### IT Free Zones
Companies operating within designated IT Free Zones benefit from:
- **0% Corporate Income Tax** (extended to 2025–2026, with renewal indicators positive)
- **10% Personal Income Tax** (vs. 20% standard rate)
- **Simplified VAT regime** for qualifying activities

Eligibility requires specific activity criteria and certification from the Ministry of High-Tech Industry.

> "Leveraging these zones requires precise corporate structuring from day one. Retrofitting an existing structure is significantly more complex than building it right from the beginning."

### Standard Rates (Non-IT-Zone)

| Tax | Rate |
|-----|------|
| Corporate Income Tax | 18% |
| VAT | 20% |
| Personal Income Tax | 20% |
| Social Security | 5% (capped) |

## Common Pitfalls

### 1. Template Employment Contracts
Armenian employment law is more protective of employees than most founders expect. Standard international templates frequently fail on notice periods, severance calculations, and non-compete enforceability.

### 2. Real Estate Title Chains
Title insurance does not exist in Armenia in the Western sense. A historical error anywhere in the title chain — even decades old — can invalidate an acquisition. Exhaustive chain-of-title due diligence is non-negotiable.

### 3. Assuming Regulatory Familiarity
The Central Bank, the State Revenue Committee, and the Ministry of High-Tech Industry each have their own documentation culture. What works with one regulator does not translate to another.

## Getting Started Right

The investors who succeed in Armenia fastest share one characteristic: they engage legal counsel **before** they incorporate, not after. The structural decisions made in the first 60 days have consequences that play out over years.

If you are considering a market entry, the most valuable conversation you can have right now is a strategic scoping call — not a document review, but a genuine discussion about your objectives and how the Armenian regulatory environment maps to them.
