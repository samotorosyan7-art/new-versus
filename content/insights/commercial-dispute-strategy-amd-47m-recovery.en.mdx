## The Challenge

Our client — a mid-size trading company — found themselves locked in a multi-jurisdictional supply chain dispute. Three separate entities across Armenia, Russia, and Cyprus were involved, and the opposing counsel had deliberately structured the claim to make asset tracing near-impossible.

The amount in dispute: AMD47 million.

The standard approach would have been a prolonged court battle lasting 18–24 months. We chose differently.

## The Versus Strategy

Before filing anything, we spent two weeks on a single objective: **mapping the opponent's credit dependencies**.

Every large enterprise has a pressure point — a line of credit, a key supplier relationship, or a regulatory deadline they cannot miss. We identified that the opposing party's primary credit facility was up for renewal within 90 days. Any court proceedings or asset freeze notices sent to their bank during that window would create maximum leverage.

> "We don't just study the law. We study the context — the opponent's incentives, constraints, and the exact moment when pressure becomes unbearable."

## The Execution

**Phase 1 — Asset Freeze**  
We filed for interim measures in all three jurisdictions simultaneously on a Monday morning. By Tuesday, the opposing party's bank accounts across all entities were frozen.

**Phase 2 — Strategic Communication**  
We did not negotiate through lawyers. We communicated directly through the channels that mattered: their bank, their primary supplier, and the regulatory body overseeing their operating license.

**Phase 3 — Settlement**  
On day two of the trial, opposing counsel approached with a full settlement offer. Our client recovered ₽47 million plus all legal costs.

## The Outcome

- Full ₽47M recovery
- Legal costs recovered
- 48-hour resolution from trial commencement
- Zero long-term business relationship damage (as requested by our client)

This case illustrates the VS Law methodology: prepare so thoroughly that the adversary's position becomes untenable before the first hearing.
