## The Challenge

A founder-led group operating across Armenia, Cyprus, and the UAE had accumulated a fragmented legal structure over eight years of rapid growth. Three operating companies, two holding entities, and a network of intercompany loans that no single advisor had ever fully mapped.

The result: significant tax friction, compliance risk in two jurisdictions, and an upcoming Series B round that required a clean corporate structure.

## The Versus Strategy

Most restructuring engagements start with the corporate structure. We started with the **exit scenario**.

If the Series B lead wanted to invest into a single clean entity, what would that entity look like? We reverse-engineered the target structure and then built a migration path backwards.

> "We build corporate architectures that anticipate the next crisis — not just resolve the current one."

## The Execution

**Week 1–2: Full audit**  
Complete mapping of all entities, intercompany obligations, IP ownership, and regulatory licences.

**Week 3–6: Structure design**  
New holding entity incorporated in Cyprus. IT Free Zone subsidiary established in Armenia for the tech operations (0% corporate tax eligible). UAE entity restructured as a regional sales office.

**Week 7–10: Migration**  
IP transferred to new holding. Intercompany loans cleared through debt-to-equity conversions. All regulatory filings submitted simultaneously.

**Week 11–13: Final clearance**  
Regulatory approvals received. Clean structure in place. Series B term sheet signed one week later.

## The Outcome

- Full restructuring executed in 90 days
- Zero tax exposure on the migration
- 15% annual efficiency gain from simplified intercompany flows
- Ready for Series B due diligence on day 91

This case is a demonstration of what precise corporate architecture looks like: not a paperwork exercise, but a strategic repositioning of the client's entire business for the next phase of growth.
